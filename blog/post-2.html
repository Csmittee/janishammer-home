The Shop That Boomed and Bust in One Year: What a Surfskate Trend Taught Me

By Janis · 3 min read

Wisdom Valley, Pattaya. I still remember watching kids carve through the ramps we'd just installed, surfskates flying off the shelves faster than we could stock them. Parents waiting in line. Classes booked solid. And somewhere in the back of my head, a quiet voice: This won't last.

But let me rewind a bit.

Working with brand owners around the world was incredible. Real relationships. Real products. And the market responded immediately. Everything sold out. Invested heavily to make the space perfect. And people came. A lot of people.

Then, in a few months, it shifted.

Competition exploded. Every shop suddenly had surfskates. People's attention drifted. There were accidents in the scene. Buy-and-sell bluffing. The trend that lifted us started collapsing under its own weight. I saw it coming ‚Äî I really did ‚Äî but I couldn't fight it. Inventory was stuck on boats. Orders arrived late. Here's what I learned: businesses built on trends are like riding a wave. You need to paddle hard to catch it, but you also need to know when to pull out before it dumps you on the rocks. Speed matters. Strategic thinking about the downside matters more. And inventory? Inventory will kill you if you don't plan for the downturn before it happens.

I don't regret opening that shop. I learned how to work with global brands, how to build a community space, how to watch kids find joy on four wheels. But the next time I ride a trend?
