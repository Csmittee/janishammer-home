A Spill of Coffee, an Expensive Capsule, and the Question That Changed Everything

By Janis · 3 min read

Coffee spilled across my paper. Brown liquid soaking into notes, blurring words I'd been staring at for hours. I wiped it up, annoyed, and looked at the capsule sitting next to me. Expensive. Beautiful packaging. The coffee inside? Good. Not great. But the price? Painful.

And that's when the question hit me: Why can't we do this?

Let me back up.

For years, most of my businesses circled around one person: my daughter, Janis. The skateboards. The surfskate shop. Even the claw machine. Everything was built around what she loved. And that made sense — Janis Lifestyle was named for her. But I started realizing something: businesses tied to one person's passion have limits. They reach certain people, sure. But they don't scale to everyone.

So I started asking a different question: What really matters for the next business?

The coffee spill was accidental. But the thought that followed wasn't. I looked at that expensive capsule and thought about the pain behind it. People who love good coffee but hate the price. Small cafes dying because they can't compete with big brands. Coffee drinkers stuck between overpriced capsules and bad alternatives.

What if I could make a great capsule at a good price? What if I could help cafes — the ones struggling to survive — turn into custom capsule brands they can sell online? What if I could build something that solves a problem I actually experience myself?

That's how Jade Project began.

Again, I'm using what I know. Supply chain. Chinese and Thai manufacturing. Finding the right supplier, building the right relationship, controlling quality. The same formula that built I-Flex, that built the claw machine partnership. But this time, it's not about a trend. It's not about my daughter's hobby. It's about a daily pain point. A product I'd buy myself. A problem I actually have.

Here's what's different this time: if this doesn't sell, I still drink the coffee. There's no inventory of unsold skateboards gathering dust. No lake permits to chase. Just a good product that solves a real problem.

Jade Project is rolling soon. And for the first time in a while, I feel like I'm building something that isn't just for one person. It's for anyone who's ever looked at an expensive capsule and thought: There has to be a better way.
